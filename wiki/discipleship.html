Tradition

We have diverse traditions based on our exegesis of the above doctrine. :

  1. We instruct that there are two general types of discipleship : narrow and broad
  2. We instruct that discipleship is necessary for all followers (read disciples) of Jesus of Nazareth.
  3. A biblical discipling relationship entails responsibility and submission; i.e. the discipler assumes responsibility for the disciple, and the disciple submits to the discipler.
  4. We instruct that the discipler meet with the disciple at least once a week for discipleship.
  5. We instruct that discipleship should entail disciples pursuing elder (for men) and eldress (for women) qualifications; (i.e. read "maturity" qualifications).
  6. We instruct that all disciples should be on the trajectory to replicate what is entrusted to them by turning and discipling others.
  7. We instruct that all disciples, in pursuit of their ultimate rabbi's will and in the context of discipleship, should seek to use, exercise, and grow the "measure(s) of grace" and spiritual gifts given to them.
  8. The goal of discipleship is to become more and more conformed to the image of Jesus the Messiah within all of our contexts and calling.
